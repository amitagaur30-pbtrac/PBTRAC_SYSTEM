Citizen Help & Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

This section helps citizens understand how to file complaints before the Punjab Transparency and Accountability Commission (PTAC).

1. Who can file a complaint before PTAC?

Any citizen who has faced delay, non-delivery, deficiency or improper delivery of a notified public service by a Government Department in Punjab may file a complaint before the Commission.

2. What information should be included in the complaint?

  • Name of the applicant
  • Complete postal address
  • Mobile number and email address
  • Name of Department concerned
  • Name of Office concerned
  • Name of the notified public service
  • Application/Acknowledgement Number
  • Date of submission of application
  • Detailed description of grievance

3. What documents should be attached?

  • Copy of original application submitted to the department
  • Acknowledgement receipt or application receipt
  • Copies of correspondence made with the department
  • Proof of delay, rejection or non-delivery of service
  • Any other supporting document relevant to the grievance

4. What relief can be sought from PTAC?

  • Direction for delivery of the public service
  • Action against the concerned official
  • Imposition of penalty for delay
  • Compensation or other relief as deemed appropriate

5. Can multiple documents be uploaded?

Yes. Citizens may upload multiple supporting documents in PDF, JPG, JPEG or PNG format.

6. How can I track my complaint?

After successful submission, a unique Complaint Number is generated. Use this number under the "Track Complaint" section to check the latest status of your complaint.

7. What are the possible complaint statuses?

  • Received
  • Assigned
  • Under Investigation
  • Resolved
  • Closed

8. Will I receive updates on my complaint?

Citizens may periodically check the complaint status online using their Complaint Number. Future SMS and Email notification facilities may also be provided.
